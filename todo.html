Bukhansan National Park

Bukhansan National Park, originally named Samgaksan(which translates to three horned mountains) is on of South Korea's twenty two national parks, and houses historical monuments such as temples and military fortresses. This park also has many nature trails and campsites which are managed under the park's governing authority, the Korean National Park Service. These trails can be accessed year-round and a 1day parking can be had for as low as 2,000 won($1.67)! Other options can be found on their website.

Bukchan Hanok Village

Bukchan Hanok Village is a historical neighborhood, so historical that the word "hanok" literally refers to a type of historical housing that dates back to the Joseon Dynasty! Even more historically, one of the most popular activities to do in Bukchon Hanok Village is to walk around in historical dress!

Gwanghwamun Square

Another Historical entry, Gwanghwamun Square, not only has the historical appeal of the previous entry, Bukchon Hanok Village, but also the entry fee of $0! Maybe make plans to pass through on a trip to another destination on this list to see the magnificent statue of King Sejong, fourth king of the Joseon Dynasty, and inventor of the Korean alphabet, Hangul.

Gwangjang Market

If ever you're hungry, and need a bite to eat, make sure to stop by Gwangjang Market! Street food, in all cultures, offers a piece of that cultural identity through taste. The locals prepare food using local ingredients, and you will not be dissapointed by the variety available. As a building lined almost form wall to wall, with several kiosks vending any korean themed street food imaginable, you'll walk away full and enamored!

Gyeongbokgung Palace

Of the five grand palaces in Seoul, Gyeongbokgung palace boasts the title of the largest of them all! With most of the original construction still in tact as well, educational tours are conducted most days of the week, as well as a historic "guard changing" ceremony twice a day!

Jjimjilbang

If your day out is coming to a close or you just want to have a slow day with some self care, hit up a local jimjilbang! Jimjilbang are essentially just convenient, inexpensive hotels with spas, video games, food, gyms, movie rentals, and beds to crash in for between 8,000-12,000 won ($5-25).

National Hangul Museum

Hangul, the korean alphabet, is regarded as having an ingenious design, and being one of the most well constructed alphabets on the planet. South koreans hold such pride and reverance for it that they even built a museum dedicated to its' history and adoption in the Joseon dynasty. If you were trying to learn, or just get a better context for how the language works, what better placecan you find?

Noraebong

Much like japanese karaoke, night lifers out on the town in Seoul can rent a room from between 6,000 and 30,000 won($5-25) and sing along to their favorites songs for a time! Establishments also often offer food and beverages, including alcohol, to keep the party going!

Seoul Tower

Similar to attractions like the Empire State building, Seoul Tower is a gorgeous landmark and is located in the Namsan district of Seoul. For just 10,000 won($8.34), tickets can be had for the elevator to the top observatory. From the observatory platform, a 360 degree view of the entire of the entire Namasan district can be seen!
